To make your transition from a live YouTube broadcast to a private in-person Q&A as seamless as possible, we’ll use **Ecamm Live Scenes**. This allows you to reconfigure your entire audio and video routing with one click, so you can stay in "teacher mode" rather than "engineer mode."

Here is how to set up your **Q&A Scene** for next week:

### 1\. Create the "Live Teaching" Scene (Your Default)

First, make sure your current setup is saved as your primary scene.

-   **Rename your current scene:** Click the name in the Scenes window and call it **"1. Teaching (Live Stream)"**.
    
-   **Verify Audio:** In the **Sound Levels** window, ensure your **RODECaster Video S** (your voice) is live and the **Meeting Owl 3** (room mic) is **Muted**.
    
-   **Verify Video:** The Remote Teacher's Zoom feed should be full-screen (which pushes to your TV).
    

---

### 2\. Create the "Q&A / Private" Scene

This is the "safe container" scene you’ll switch to after ending the stream.

1.  **Duplicate the Scene:** Click the gear icon next to your "Teaching" scene and select **Duplicate**.
    
2.  **Rename it:** Call this one **"2. Q&A (Private)"**.
    
3.  **Adjust the Audio (Crucial):**
    
    -   In the **Sound Levels** window for *this scene only*:
        
    -   **Mute** the RODECaster Video S (since the Owl is about to become the room mic).
        
    -   **Unmute** the Meeting Owl 3.
        
    -   *Note: Ensure the Remote Guest fader remains UP so her voice still comes through the Owl speaker.*
        

---

### 3\. The "Meditation-Friendly" Workflow

When you are ready to stop the YouTube stream and move to the shared circle:

1.  **Click "Finish"** in Ecamm to end the YouTube broadcast.
    
2.  **Click the "2. Q&A (Private)" Scene.**
    
    -   **Instantly:** Your high-end RODE mic is off (protecting your privacy as you move around).
        
    -   **Instantly:** The Owl’s 360° mics are live, picking up the sangha members.
        
    -   **Continuously:** The teacher’s face stays on the TV and her voice stays in the Owl.
        

---

### One Final Pro-Tip for Next Week

Since you'll be teaching, you might not want to reach for your mouse. You can assign a **Keyboard Shortcut** to these scenes:

-   Go to the **Scenes** menu at the top of your Mac.
    
-   Find your "Q&A" scene.
    
-   Hold **Option (⌥)** and click the scene name to assign a key (like the number `2`).
    

Now, you can just tap a single key on your keyboard to transition the entire room into "Private Mode."

### 🛠 Troubleshooting: "What if...?"

| Problem | Likely Cause | Quick Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Teacher echoes herself** | Owl & Ecamm "fighting." | Ensure **Echo Cancellation** is **OFF** in Ecamm Preferences. |
| **Teacher is still too quiet** | Fader is maxed but quiet. | Increase **Pre-gain** in the **AULimiter** filter we added. |
| **YouTube hears an echo** | Your RODE mic is too hot. | Lower your RODE mic fader slightly or move it further from the Owl. |

| **Sound effects are silent** | Monitor routing. | In **Sound Levels**, make sure the **Speaker icon** is blue for "Sound Effects." |

### The "Theater-Proof" Limiter Setup

Instead of a manual toggle, you can set the **AULimiter** to act as an automatic "safety net" for her voice:

1. **Set the Pre-gain High (+8dB to +12dB):** This "drags" her quietest whispers up so the sangha in the back of the room can hear her.
    
2. **The Limiter Logic:** The "limiter" part of the plugin acts as a hard ceiling. If she suddenly gets excited or moves closer to the mic, the plugin won't let her audio "clip" or distort. It just keeps the volume at a consistent, safe level.
    
3. **No Toggle Needed:** Because it’s an automatic process, you don't need a keyboard shortcut. It will boost her when she's quiet and "squash" her back down if she gets loud.

### 🧘 The "Guest Rescue" Filter Stack

(Apply these to the **Guest Channel** in the Ecamm Sound Levels window, top-to-bottom.)

---

### 1\. AUHighPassFilter (The "Cleaner")

-   **Cutoff Frequency:** **100Hz**
    
-   **The Goal:** Kill the sub-bass rumble, desk thumps, and phone vibrations *before* they hit the boost.
    

---

### 2\. AUGraphicEQ (The "Shaper")

-   **20Hz to 100Hz:** **\-20dB** (The "Floor")
    
-   **125Hz to 160Hz:** **\-10dB** (The "Boxy" Cut)
    
-   **200Hz to 400Hz:** **+3dB to +5dB** (The "Warmth/Body" Boost)
    
-   **400Hz to 1.6kHz:** **0dB** (Neutral)
    
-   **2kHz to 4kHz:** **+2dB** (The "Consonant/Clarity" Boost)
    
-   **5kHz to 20kHz:** **0dB** (Neutral)
    

---

### 3\. AUPeakLimiter (The "Safety & Master Volume")

-   **Pre-gain:** **+10dB** (The "Rescue" boost)
    
-   **Attack:** **2ms** (The "Shield" against pops)
    
-   **Release:** **40ms** (The "Cushion" for natural speech)
