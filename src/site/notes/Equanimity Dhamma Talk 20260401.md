## **Dhamma Talk: The Clearing and the Swells**

**Time:** 15 Minutes

**Theme:** Equanimity (*Upekkha*) as Active Presence

### **I. Opening: The "Winging It" Practice (2 Minutes)**

-   **Transparency:** Acknowledge stepping in for Amy while recovering yourself.
    
-   **The Point:** This isn't a lecture from a pedestal; it’s a report from the field. Equanimity isn't waiting for the "perfect" conditions—it’s how we relate to *these* conditions.
    

---

### **II. Internal Preparation: "Clearing" (4 Minutes)**

-   **Context:** Before we can meet the world, we have to stop fighting it.
    
-   **Poem: *Clearing* by Martha Postlethwaite**

    > the whole world
    > 
    > or do anything grandiose.

    > a clearing

    >

### **III. The Sutta Anchor: Being Like the Earth (4 Minutes)**

-   **Context:** How do we hold what falls into our "cupped hands"?
    
-   **Sutta: *Maha-Rahulovada Sutta* (MN 62)**
    
    > "Rahula, develop meditation like the earth. For when you develop meditation like the earth, arisen agreeable and disagreeable sensory impressions will not take charge of your mind and stay there. Just as people throw clean things and dirty things, excrement, urine, spit, pus, and blood on the earth, but the earth is not repelled, humiliated, or disgusted... in the same way, develop meditation like the earth."
    
-   **The Insight:** The earth doesn't argue with what lands on it. It has the "capacity" to hold it all without being diminished.
    

---

### **IV. The Embodiment: "The Little Duck" (5 Minutes)**

-   **Context:** The earth is our stability; the duck is our movement through the world’s "swells."
    
-   **Poem: *The Little Duck* by Donald C. Babcock**
    
    > Now we are ready to look at something pretty special.
    > 
    > It is a duck riding the ocean a hundred feet beyond the surf.
    > 
    > No, it isn’t a gull.
    > 
    > A gull always has a raucous touch about him.
    > 
    > This is some sort of duck, and he cuddles in the swells.
    > 
    > He isn’t fighting anything, but he isn’t helpless either.
    > 
    > He is simply at home.
    > 
    > He isn’t doing anything.
    > 
    > He is just being there.
    > 
    > The sea is doing everything.
    > 
    > It is lifting him and lowering him.
    > 
    > It is trying to drown him, only it can’t.
    > 
    > It is trying to wash him away, only it can’t.
    > 
    > It is doing its worst, and it is doing its best.
    > 
    > And the little duck?
    > 
    > He is just being a duck.
    > 
    > He is perfectly safe.
    > 
    > He is perfectly at home.
    

---

### **V. Closing: Transition to Sit**

-   **The "Take-Home":** "He isn't fighting anything, but he isn't helpless either."
    
-   **The Invitation:** Invite the sangha to find that "at home" quality in the upcoming sit—letting the breath be the tide, and the mind be the earth.
